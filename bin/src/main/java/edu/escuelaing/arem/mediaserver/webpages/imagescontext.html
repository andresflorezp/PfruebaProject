Los Angeles:
Officially the City of Los Angeles, known colloquially by its initials L.A.) is the second-most

Camilo Cienfuegos:
Camilo Cienfuegos Gorriaran (6 February 1932 – 28 October 1959) was a Cuban revolutionary born
in Havana. Along with Fidel Castro, Che Guevara, Juan Almeida Bosque, and Raúl Castro, he was a
member of the 1956 Granma expedition, which launched Fidel Castro's armed insurgency against the
government of Cuban dictator Fulgencio Batista. He became one of Castro's top guerilla commanders,
known as the "Hero of Yaguajay" after winning a key battle of the Cuban Revolution. His signature
weapon was the Thompson submachine gun.



Jean-Michel Basquiat:
Jean-Michel Basquiat (December 22, 1960 – August 12, 1988) was an American artist of Haitian and
Puerto Rican descent. Basquiat first achieved fame as part of SAMO, an informal graffiti duo who
wrote enigmatic epigrams in the cultural hotbed of the Lower East Side of Manhattan during the
late 1970s where the hip hop, punk, and street art cultures had coalesced. By the 1980s, he was
exhibiting his neo-expressionist paintings in galleries and museums internationally. The Whitney
Museum of American Art held a retrospective of his art in 1992.
